# Coaching Carousel

At the end of each season, AI-controlled head coaches evaluate their situations and may leave their programs. The coaching carousel runs during the offseason phase.

## Coach personas

Every AI head coach has a persona that governs their career decisions:

| Persona | Behavior |
|---------|---------|
| **Loyalist** | Stays long-term. Resistant to outside offers. Builds deep roots. |
| **Builder** | Targets rebuilding programs. Motivated by turning programs around — may leave once rebuilt. |
| **Climber** | Chases prestige upgrades. Leaves for better programs when the opportunity arises. |
| **Wanderer** | Unpredictable. High variance — anything can happen. |

## The carousel cycle

After bowl season:

1. **Evaluation** — Programs evaluate their coach based on wins, conference performance, bowl results, and prestige expectations
2. **Firings** — Underperforming coaches at high-expectation programs are let go
3. **Voluntary departures** — Climbers and Wanderers may leave for better opportunities
4. **Open positions** — Vacancies are filled from available coaches; persona influences which jobs coaches pursue
5. **Hiring** — Programs hire based on prestige tier and candidate pool

## Prestige and expectations

Programs are rated 1–100. High-prestige programs (80+) fire faster and attract better candidates. A coach who consistently wins at a mid-prestige school may draw offers from elite programs — whether they leave depends on their persona.

## Impact on your opponents

When a new coach takes over a program:
- Their offensive/defensive philosophies may differ from the previous staff
- Recruiting tendencies shift to match the new coach's system
- Inherited rosters may not fit the new scheme

Programs you beat comfortably one year may arrive with a stronger staff the next — the carousel adds long-term unpredictability to the simulation.
