# Conference Realignment

Conference realignment allows programs to move between conferences over time, reflecting the ongoing shifts in college football's landscape.

## How realignment happens

Realignment occurs during the offseason. Programs evaluate their conference fit and may pursue a move; conferences may also approach programs that fit their expansion needs.

Factors driving realignment:
- **Program prestige** — higher-prestige programs attract P4 conference interest
- **Market size** — programs in large media markets are attractive to conferences
- **Conference needs** — conferences seeking expansion prioritize geography and prestige fit

## Teams in transition

When a move is confirmed, the program enters a **transitioning** state for the offseason:
- They are filtered out of the League Hub team picker — not available for coach assignment during transition
- Existing schedule commitments are honored
- They join the new conference at the start of the following season

## Scheduling impact

When a program changes conferences, the following season's schedule adjusts:
- Conference game count changes to match the new conference's requirements
- Non-conference slots fill in the remainder
- Conference rivalry games tied to previous membership may not carry over

## Automatic process

Realignment runs automatically as part of the simulation — the commissioner does not manually trigger individual moves. The system evaluates fits and initiates transitions based on the simulation's logic each offseason.
