# NFL Draft & Prestige

At the end of each season, eligible players leave for the NFL. Draft success feeds back into program prestige over time.

## Who leaves

**Seniors** — All seniors complete their eligibility after bowl season and depart. High-rated seniors are evaluated for draft selection.

**Underclassmen who declare** — Junior players rated above a threshold may declare early. Once declared, they leave regardless of whether they're selected.

## How draft selection works

Players are evaluated based on overall rating and position-specific attributes. Premium positions — QB, edge rusher, cornerback, offensive tackle — are weighted more heavily in early rounds. The draft is simulated across all programs: a player's selection position depends on their rating relative to the full pool of eligible players.

## Prestige impact

Programs that consistently produce NFL Draft picks gain prestige:

| Selection tier | Prestige impact |
|---------------|----------------|
| Rounds 1–3 | Significant boost |
| Rounds 4–7 | Moderate boost |
| Undrafted free agent | Small boost |

Draft production compounds over multiple seasons. A program that regularly sends players to the NFL earns prestige faster than one with isolated standout classes.

## Prestige scale

Prestige runs 1–100 and changes slowly. A single good recruiting class or draft year won't swing it dramatically — it accumulates across seasons and declines gradually with poor performance.

High-prestige programs (80+):
- Attract 4- and 5-star recruits more easily
- Draw Climber coaches during the carousel
- Are first targets during conference realignment expansion
