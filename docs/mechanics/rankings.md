# Rankings

The AP Poll ranks the top 25 teams after each week of the regular season and postseason. Rankings affect bowl selection, CFP qualification, and recruiting pull.

## How the poll is calculated

Rankings update after each week's simulation. The algorithm weighs:

- **Win-loss record** — the primary factor
- **Strength of schedule** — wins against ranked or high-prestige opponents count for more
- **Margin of victory** — blowout wins over quality opponents carry more weight than narrow wins
- **Loss recency** — a team that lost early and won every game since ranks higher than a team with a recent loss
- **Preseason prestige** — high-prestige programs start with poll goodwill and require losses to fall out

## Poll movement

A team can:
- **Rise** — win convincingly, especially against ranked opponents
- **Fall** — lose, or win narrowly against a weak opponent while others win impressively
- **Drop out** — a loss typically knocks a team out of the top 25 unless they were highly ranked

## Impact of rankings

**Bowl selection** — Higher-ranked teams receive better bowl bids.

**CFP bracket** — Top 12 teams qualify. Seeds 1–4 receive first-round byes; seeds 5–12 play in the first round.

**Recruiting** — Breaking into the top 25 noticeably improves interest from prestige-focused recruits.

**Prestige** — Sustained top-25 presence builds program prestige over multiple seasons.

## Viewing rankings

The Rankings page in the coach web portal shows the current top 25 with each team's record. It updates automatically when the commissioner advances the week.
