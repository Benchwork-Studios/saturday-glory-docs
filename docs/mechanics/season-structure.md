# Season Structure

Saturday Glory runs in a continuous multi-year cycle. Each season passes through six phases in order, with the commissioner advancing the league week by week.

## The season cycle

```
Offseason → Spring Practice → Fall Camp → Regular Season → Conference Championships → Bowl Season → Offseason → ...
```

| Phase | Weeks | What's happening |
|-------|-------|-----------------|
| **Offseason** | 8 | Recruiting, transfer portal, coaching carousel, NFL draft |
| **Spring Practice** | 6 | Roster competition, depth chart formation |
| **Fall Camp** | 4 | Final prep, depth chart finalization |
| **Regular Season** | 12 | Weekly game simulation, active recruiting |
| **Conference Championships** | 1 | Division winners compete for conference titles |
| **Bowl Season** | 4 | Postseason bowl matchups |

## Regular season schedule

Every FBS team plays 12 games per regular season. Conference game totals vary by conference size:

| Conference tier | Conference games |
|----------------|-----------------|
| Large P4 (SEC, Big Ten, Big 12, ACC) | 9 |
| Mid-size G6 (AAC, Sun Belt, MAC) | 8 |
| Small conferences (Mountain West, Pac-12, C-USA) | 7 |

The remaining games are non-conference matchups. Each team targets 6 home games per season.

## Postseason

**Conference Championships** — Division winners (in conferences with divisions) advance to their conference title game. One week, one game.

**Bowl Season** — Teams with 6 or more wins are bowl eligible. Bowl matchups are generated based on conference tie-ins and team records. Bowl season runs 4 weeks, with games spread across the window.

**College Football Playoff** — The top 12 teams by ranking qualify for the CFP. Seeds 1–4 receive first-round byes. The bracket plays through the bowl season window.

## Week advancement

The commissioner advances weeks manually. There is no automatic progression — each advance is a deliberate action after all coaches have submitted their plans and the commissioner runs the simulation.

The season calendar does not correspond to real-world dates. A season can take as long as your league needs.
