# Roster & Depth Chart

## Roster page

The Roster page shows every player on your team — scholarship players and walk-ons. For each player you can see:

- **Name and position**
- **Class year** — FR, SO, JR, SR, or GR (graduate year)
- **Star rating** — the recruiting rating the player had when they signed
- **Overall rating** — current calculated rating based on all position-specific attributes
- **Status** — healthy, injured, or suspended

Rosters are managed in the Mac app by the commissioner. As a coach on the web portal, the roster is read-only — you can view it but not edit it.

## Depth Chart page

The Depth Chart shows your starters and backups organized by position unit. It answers the question: who is lining up where?

Each position shows:
- **Starter** — the primary player at that spot
- **Backup** — the second-string player
- **Third string** — shown where applicable

Depth charts are also managed in the Mac app and are read-only in the web portal.

## Player Attributes by Position

Every player is evaluated across a set of attributes specific to their position. These attributes roll up into the player's **Overall Rating** and drive the qualitative scouting analysis shown on each depth chart row.

### Quarterback (QB)
| Attribute | What it measures |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Arm Accuracy | Ball placement and timing on short, intermediate, and deep routes |
| Arm Strength | Ability to drive the ball downfield and attack all three levels |
| Speed | Pocket mobility and scramble ability |
| Elusiveness | Ability to avoid pressure and extend plays outside the pocket |

### Running Back (RB)
| Attribute | What it measures |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Strength | Power between the tackles and ability to fall forward |
| Break Tackle | Ability to shed arm tackles and keep running on contact |
| Speed | Burst through the hole and top-end open-field speed |
| Elusiveness | Cutting and change of direction in space |
| Catching | Reliability as a receiving option out of the backfield |

### Fullback (FB)
| Attribute | What it measures |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Strength | Power as a short-yardage ball carrier |
| Break Tackle | Goal-line toughness and ability to absorb contact |
| Run Blocking | Lead blocking ability and effectiveness at the point of attack |
| Catching | Usefulness as a checkdown or slot receiving option |

### Wide Receiver (WR)
| Attribute | What it measures |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Route Running | Ability to get off the line, create separation with cuts |
| Catching | Hands in traffic and reliability on catchable balls |
| Speed | Deep threat ability and separation downfield |

### Tight End (TE)
| Attribute | What it measures |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Catching | Pass-catching reliability on routes and in traffic |
| Route Running | Ability to win against linebackers and safeties |
| Run Blocking | Effectiveness at the point of attack in the run game |
| Elusiveness | Athleticism to line up in the slot and create mismatches |

### Offensive Line (OT / OG / C)
| Attribute | What it measures |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Pass Blocking | Ability to anchor, mirror, and hold up against pass rushers |
| Run Blocking | Ability to generate movement and drive defenders off the ball |

### Defensive End / Defensive Tackle (DE / DT)
| Attribute | What it measures |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Pass Rush | Get-off and hand technique to beat blockers one-on-one |
| Block Shedding | Ability to disengage from blocks and pursue the ball carrier |
| Tackling | Pursuit tackling and stopping the run past the line |
| Strength | Ability to set the edge and hold ground against power |
| Play Recognition | Pre-snap reads and ability to diagnose runs and screens quickly |

### Linebacker (LB)
| Attribute | What it measures |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Tackling | Downhill run-stopping and open-field tackle consistency |
| Hit Power | Physical impact in the run game and on tackles |
| Strength | Ability to shed blocks and hold the box |
| Man Coverage | Ability to match up with backs and tight ends in man |
| Zone Coverage | Ability to hold assignments in hook-curl and hook-flat zones |
| Play Recognition | Reading the offense pre-snap and diagnosing quickly |

### Cornerback (CB)
| Attribute | What it measures |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Man Coverage | Press and off-man technique against wide receivers |
| Zone Coverage | Positioning and discipline in zone assignments |
| Play Recognition | Reading the quarterback and breaking on routes |
| Tackling | Run-support effectiveness and open-field tackle reliability |
| Hit Power | Willingness and effectiveness in physical coverage and run support |

### Safety (S)
| Attribute | What it measures |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Man Coverage | Ability to cover backs, tight ends, and slot receivers |
| Zone Coverage | Range and positioning in two-deep or single-high looks |
| Play Recognition | Pre-snap reads, run/pass diagnosis, and rotation timing |
| Tackling | Open-field tackle reliability as a box safety |
| Hit Power | Physical presence near the line of scrimmage |

### Kicker / Punter (K / P)
| Attribute | What it measures |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Kick Accuracy | Consistency on field goals and placement on punts |
| Kick Power | Leg strength for long field goals and deep kickoff coverage |

## Using the roster to inform your game plan

The roster and depth chart are your reference when setting your weekly game plan. For example:

- If your starting QB is injured, check the depth chart to see who's next
- If your running back corps is deep and highly rated, leaning run-heavy may be effective
- A weak offensive line (low ratings across OT, OG, C) might favor quick-pass schemes over run blocking

The simulation engine uses the depth chart to determine which players participate in each game.

## Class year progression

At the end of each season, players advance a class year:
- FR → SO → JR → SR → graduation
- Seniors graduate and leave the roster
- Graduate year (GR) players have used a redshirt year and have one additional season

Redshirted players retain their class year for one additional season. The commissioner controls redshirt decisions in the Mac app.
