# Roster & Depth Chart

## Roster page

The Roster page shows every player on your team — scholarship players and walk-ons. For each player you can see:

- **Name and position**
- **Class year** — FR, SO, JR, SR, or GR (graduate year)
- **Star rating** — the recruiting rating the player had when they signed
- **Overall rating** — current calculated rating based on all position-specific attributes
- **Status** — healthy, injured, or suspended

Rosters are managed in the Mac app by the commissioner. As a coach on the web portal, the roster is read-only — you can view it but not edit it.

## Depth Chart page

The Depth Chart shows your starters and backups organized by position unit. It answers the question: who is lining up where?

Each position shows:
- **Starter** — the primary player at that spot
- **Backup** — the second-string player
- **Third string** — shown where applicable

Depth charts are also managed in the Mac app and are read-only in the web portal.

## Using the roster to inform your game plan

The roster and depth chart are your reference when setting your weekly game plan. For example:

- If your starting QB is injured, check the depth chart to see who's next
- If your running back corps is deep and highly rated, leaning run-heavy may be effective
- A weak offensive line (low ratings across OT, OG, C) might favor quick-pass schemes over run blocking

The simulation engine uses the depth chart to determine which players participate in each game.

## Class year progression

At the end of each season, players advance a class year:
- FR → SO → JR → SR → graduation
- Seniors graduate and leave the roster
- Graduate year (GR) players have used a redshirt year and have one additional season

Redshirted players retain their class year for one additional season. The commissioner controls redshirt decisions in the Mac app.
